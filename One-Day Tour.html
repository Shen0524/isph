🗿 Yehliu Geopark

Yehliu is a cape extending from the Datun Mountain into the sea. When viewed from Jinshan, it resembles a giant turtle diving into the sea, hence it's also called "Yehliu Turtle." Through wave erosion, rock weathering, and crustal movement, it has created various unique landscapes including sea caves, candle-shaped rocks, mushroom rocks, tofu rocks, honeycomb rocks, potholes, and solution pans. The Queen's Head, Fairy's Shoe, and Candlestick Rock are internationally famous sea erosion wonders. The statue of Lin Tian-chen in the area commemorates his heroic deed of sacrificing his life to save others.

🏮 Jiufen Old Street

A famous tourist old street in Ruifang District, New Taipei City, Taiwan, known for its unique hillside terrain, nostalgic architectural style, and rich food culture. Jiufen Old Street is mainly concentrated on Jishan Street, Shuqi Road, and Qingbian Road, and is one of Taiwan's most popular tourist attractions. The narrow alleyways are lined with traditional red lanterns and teahouses, creating a magical atmosphere reminiscent of scenes from "Spirited Away."
