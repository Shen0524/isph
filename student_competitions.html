ISPH 2026 Annual Conference Student Competitions

To encourage graduate student participation and promote academic exchange in precision health, the International Society for Precision Health (ISPH) will organize student competitions during the Annual Conference.

Competition Categories: Doctoral Oral Presentation Competition  |  Master’s Poster Presentation Competition

During the ISPH Annual Conference, two student competitions will be held to recognize outstanding research contributions by graduate students:

  • Doctoral Oral Presentation Competition
  • Master’s Poster Presentation Competition

Eligibility

Master’s and doctoral students currently enrolled at accredited colleges or universities.

Submission Requirement

Participants must submit an abstract to the Annual Conference as the first author.

Entry Limit

Each participant may submit one entry only.

Poster Display Dates

Master’s posters must be displayed on October 21–22.

1. Purpose

The Student Competitions are intended to encourage active participation by graduate students, provide opportunities to present research and receive professional feedback, and recognize outstanding young scholars in the field of precision health.

2. Organizer

International Society for Precision Health (ISPH)

3. Eligibility

  1. The Doctoral Oral Presentation Competition is open to students currently enrolled in a doctoral program.
  2. The Master’s Poster Presentation Competition is open to students currently enrolled in a master’s program.
  3. All participants must submit an abstract to the ISPH Annual Conference as the first author.
  4. Each participant may submit only one entry.
  5. Proof of student status must be provided upon request.
  6. The same work may not be submitted to more than one competition category.

4. Registration

  1. Participants must complete both abstract submission and competition registration by the official Annual Conference deadline.
  2. All required registration materials must be submitted according to conference instructions.
  3. Incomplete applications or applications that do not meet the eligibility requirements will not be accepted.
  4. Final acceptance is subject to review and approval by the organizer.

5. Competition Format

5.1 Doctoral Oral Presentation Competition

  1. This competition will be conducted through on-site oral presentations and Q&A.
  2. Participants must present according to the official conference schedule, venue, and instructions announced by the organizer.
  3. Presentation time and Q&A time will be announced separately by the organizer.
  4. Participants who fail to appear at the assigned time will be regarded as having withdrawn from the competition.

5.2 Master’s Poster Presentation Competition

  1. This competition will be conducted through on-site poster display and oral explanation with Q&A.
  2. Poster size must be 80 cm (W) × 100 cm (H).
  3. Posters must be displayed during October 21–22 at the designated location assigned by the organizer.
  4. Posters must remain on display throughout the required period and may not be removed early without permission.
  5. Participants must be present during the assigned judging period to introduce their work and answer questions from the judges.
  6. Participants who fail to post their poster on time or fail to appear during the assigned judging period will be regarded as having withdrawn from the competition.

6. Review Process

  1. A review committee composed of experts and scholars in relevant fields will be appointed by ISPH.
  2. All entries will be evaluated in a fair and professional manner.
  3. The decisions of the review committee shall be final.

7. Evaluation Criteria

7.1 Doctoral Oral Presentation Competition

  • Significance and originality of the research topic
  • Rigor of study design and methodology
  • Completeness of results analysis and discussion
  • Quality of presentation and communication skills
  • Performance during Q&A

7.2 Master’s Poster Presentation Competition

  • Academic or practical value of the research topic
  • Completeness of methods and results
  • Organization and visual quality of the poster
  • Clarity of on-site explanation
  • Performance during Q&A

8. Awards

Doctoral Oral Presentation Competition

  • First Prize: USD 500
  • Second Prize: USD 300
  • Third Prize: USD 100

Master’s Poster Presentation Competition

  • First Prize: USD 300
  • Second Prize: USD 200
  • Third Prize: USD 100

Awards will be granted on a merit basis. The review committee reserves the right not to award a prize if no entry meets the required standard. Winners may also receive a certificate or award plaque. Award payments will be subject to applicable tax and administrative regulations.

9. Important Notes

  1. Submitted work must be the original work of the participant. Any plagiarism, falsification, infringement of intellectual property rights, or other violation of academic ethics will result in disqualification.
  2. If any false statement or ineligible status is identified, the organizer reserves the right to cancel participation or awards.
  3. By registering for the competition, participants agree to comply with these Guidelines and all additional conference regulations announced by the organizer.

10. Final Provision

Any matters not specified in these Guidelines shall be interpreted and determined by the organizer.

For additional conference updates, registration details, and abstract submission information, please refer to the official ISPH Annual Conference website.
